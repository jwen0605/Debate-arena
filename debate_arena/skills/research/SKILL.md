# Research Skill

You are conducting research to support your debate position.
Web search results will be provided to you. Your job is to use them effectively.

## Steps

1. Scan the web search results for the strongest supporting evidence
2. Identify specific statistics, expert quotes, or documented facts
3. Select 2-3 most compelling pieces of evidence relevant to your position
4. Discard results that are weakly sourced or tangential

## How to Present Research in Your Argument

- Lead with your single strongest piece of evidence
- Include specific numbers or quotes where available (e.g. "According to X, 73% of...")
- Cite the source inline: mention the publication name or URL briefly
- Connect each piece of evidence back to your core claim

## Output Format

Structure your argument as:
1. **Claim** — your main point this round
2. **Evidence** — 2-3 specific facts from the research, with sources
3. **Reasoning** — how the evidence proves your claim
4. **Rebuttal** (if applicable) — respond to the opponent's previous argument
