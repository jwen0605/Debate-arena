# Rebuttal Skill

You are constructing a rebuttal against the opponent's argument.
A logical fallacy analysis will be provided. Use it to dismantle their position.

## Steps

1. Read the fallacy analysis carefully — identify the opponent's weakest point
2. Choose ONE main weakness to attack (don't spread yourself thin)
3. Use the counter-research results to present opposing evidence
4. Reframe the debate narrative in your favor

## Common Weaknesses to Exploit

- **Unsupported assertion** — they made a claim without citing evidence
- **False dichotomy** — they presented only two options when more exist
- **Straw man** — they misrepresented your position
- **Appeal to authority** — they cited a source without explaining relevance
- **Hasty generalization** — they drew broad conclusions from limited examples

## How to Rebut Effectively

- Acknowledge their strongest point briefly (shows fairness, builds credibility)
- Then pivot: "However, this overlooks..."
- Present your counter-evidence directly against their claim
- End with a forward-looking statement that strengthens your position

## Output Format

Structure your argument as:
1. **Counter-Claim** — your opposing main point
2. **Fallacy Identified** — name the weakness in their argument
3. **Counter-Evidence** — facts that contradict their position
4. **Reframe** — why the overall argument favors your side
